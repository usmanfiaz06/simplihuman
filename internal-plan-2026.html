Equity Structure: Honoring Agreements Fairly

Two service partners hold equity for work contributions. Equity should be honored at the agreed valuation when contracts were signed — calculated proportionally to actual work delivered, not necessarily the full allocated percentage.

Stakeholder % Type Contribution Model

Saima Salman 25.3% Cash + Sweat Product, R&D, domain (psych) + cash
Doruk Sardag 14.0% Cash Only Financial investment
Shean 4.7% Cash Only Financial investment
Design Peeps 10.0% Sweat Only All design — 100% in-kind vs equity
Ambeego 5.0%* Sweat (Hybrid) Dev work — 50% cash + 50% vs equity

*Ambeego's 5% is against the equity portion only (they also receive 50% cash payment for invoices)

Equity Calculation Principle

Honor at Agreed Valuation, Proportional to Work Done

Step 1: Take the valuation at the time the contract was signed
Step 2: Calculate the total scope of work agreed upon
Step 3: Calculate the actual work delivered to date
Step 4: Equity earned = (Work Delivered / Total Agreed Scope) × Allocated %

Example: If Design Peeps was allocated 10% for a defined scope, and 70% of that scope has been delivered, earned equity = 7% (not automatically 10%).

Key Differences from Previous Version

This is NOT a 4-Year Vesting Schedule

The partner agreements state that services will be provided until investment or revenue reaches a stage where it is feasible to onboard an in-house team. Partners then continue as leads — Ambeego as CTO leading dev, Design Peeps as Design Lead. This is milestone-based, not time-based.

Different Models for Each Partner

Design Peeps: 100% work delivered in-kind against equity. All design work is unpaid — compensated entirely through equity allocation.

Ambeego: Hybrid model. Charges 50% of invoice as cash (paid), and the remaining 50% is accumulated against equity. Both cash and equity components are real and tracked.

Recommended Resolution Framework

1 Audit Work Delivered

Document all deliverables from both partners. Match against original scope agreement. Calculate % of scope completed.

2 Calculate Earned Equity

Proportional to work done at the valuation agreed when the contract was signed. Not necessarily the full 10% or 5%.

3 Define Ongoing Terms

Future work continues under the same agreement until transition milestone is reached. Remaining equity vests as additional work is delivered.

4 Formalize in Shareholders' Agreement

Document earned equity, ongoing terms, transition milestone, and leadership roles post-transition. Legal cost: ~$2K–$3K.

Design Work: The Full Picture

Design is far more than "35 screens." It encompasses deep research, extensive iteration cycles, A/B variant creation, marketing/brand support, ongoing bug fixes, and continuous product evolution. Here is the true scope.

What Design Actually Involves

UX Research & Discovery

User interviews, competitor analysis, persona refinement, journey mapping, information architecture. Foundation for every design decision.

Ongoing
UI Screen Design & Iterations

Not one-and-done. Each screen goes through 3–5 iteration rounds based on feedback, testing, and stakeholder input. 35 screens × 4 avg iterations = 140+ design versions.

High Effort
A/B Test Variants & Prototyping

Each experiment needs 2–3 design variants. 6 planned A/B tests = 12–18 additional variant designs, each with its own flow logic and prototyping.

Per Experiment
Marketing & Brand Design

App store screenshots, social media creatives, ad assets (TikTok, Instagram, Snapchat), pitch deck visuals, website updates, email templates, launch assets.

Continuous
Bug Fixes & Design QA

Regular fixes for visual bugs, responsive issues, dark mode problems, accessibility gaps, animation polish, and platform-specific (iOS vs Android) adjustments.

Weekly
Design System Maintenance

Component library, style guide, token management, icon system, illustration system. Keeps everything consistent as the product grows.

Confidential Risks

Risk Register & Mitigations

HIGH Capital runs out before traction

Founder contributions cover 2026 operating costs but additional capital gap ($62K–$105K) isn't fully funded.

Mitigation: Phase spending strictly. Marketing only scales with proven metrics. Apply for grants. Lean on partner model (85% cheaper than in-house). Prioritize revenue-generating features.

HIGH Product fixes don't move PMF

We fix onboarding + paywall but PMF stays <40%. Users still see app as "nice-to-have."

Mitigation: Rapid A/B tests with 2-week cohort windows. If PMF doesn't move by Q3, pivot to AI chat depth over breadth. Saima leads domain-specific research pivots.

MED Partner capacity / dedication risk

Design Peeps or Ambeego can't dedicate enough hours. Delays cascade across the roadmap.

Mitigation: Formalize weekly hour commitments in updated agreements. Set clear sprint goals. Have backup freelancer pipeline identified. Equity earned is proportional to output delivered.

MED Equity disagreement stalls progress

Resolution of sweat equity takes too long or creates founder conflict.

Mitigation: Resolve in Q1 (hard deadline). Use work-delivered calculation as the basis. Honor agreed valuation. Bring in neutral advisor if needed. Budget $2–3K for legal.

MED CPI inflation in Tier-1 markets

CPIs rising as more wellness apps enter. Our $0.90 blended target may be optimistic.

Mitigation: Heavy organic investment (35% of effort). Shift spend to MENA (60% lower CPIs). University partnerships as zero-CPI channel. Design Peeps creates high-quality ad creatives to improve conversion.

LOW Guest mode increases anonymous churn

Users stay anonymous, never convert, can't be re-engaged.

Mitigation: Progressive signup nudges after 2 sessions. Gate premium + Echo behind account creation. Track guest→registered conversion rate as key metric.
