Confidential Equity

Equity Structure: Resolving Sweat vs Cash

Two partners (Design Peeps at 10% and Ambeego at 5%) hold equity for work contributions. As cash-contributing co-founders invest, we need a fair framework to balance sweat equity vs cash equity.

The Core Problem

Double-counting risk: If a partner contributes both work (sweat) AND cash, they receive value twice for overlapping effort. Cash-only founders may feel shortchanged. Work-only partners may feel devalued if their equity dilutes with cash rounds.

Current Equity Positions

Partner Equity % Type Contribution
Co-Founders (5) 85% Cash + Sweat Strategy, ops, clinical, design, tech + cash investment
Design Peeps 10% Sweat Only UI/UX design work (completed)
Ambeego 5% Sweat Only Development work (completed)

Industry Standard: Slicing Pie Model

Mike Moyer's Slicing Pie is the most widely-used framework for resolving this exact scenario in early-stage startups:

  • All contributions are converted to a single unit: "slices"
  • Cash contributions are valued at 2-4x multiplier (cash is riskier and more scarce)
  • Sweat contributions are valued at fair market rate x time spent
  • Each person's equity = their slices / total slices
  • Dynamic: Equity adjusts as new contributions are made

Recommended Resolution Options

A

Fixed Equity + Vesting (Recommended)

Honor the agreed 10% and 5% but subject to 4-year vesting with 1-year cliff. Work already done counts toward cliff. If they stop contributing, unvested shares return to pool.

If they also invest cash: Cash buys additional shares from a new option pool at the current valuation — separate from sweat equity. No double-counting because sweat = past work, cash = new capital.
B

Convert to Contractor + Reduced Equity

Retrospectively value their work at market rate (e.g., design work = $15K, dev work = $8K). Convert to: reduced equity (e.g., 6% + 3%) + cash payment for remainder. Clean break if they're not ongoing contributors.

C

Slicing Pie Full Recalculation

Recalculate all equity from scratch using fair market rates for all contributions (time, cash, IP, assets). Most fair but most complex. Requires agreement from all parties on rates.

Key Principles (Any Option)

  • Cash gets a premium: Industry standard is 2-4x multiplier on cash vs sweat (cash carries more risk and opportunity cost)
  • Vesting protects everyone: Nobody walks away with equity for uncommitted future work
  • Ongoing work ≠ free: Future design/dev work should be compensated separately (salary or additional equity grants), not assumed under original equity deal
  • Get it in writing: Any resolution must be documented in a formal shareholders' agreement before the seed round closes

Confidential Founder Capital

Founder Cash Contributions: Pro-Rata Model

Co-founders contributing cash in proportion to their equity share. This maintains fairness and prevents dilution disputes.

How Pro-Rata Founder Investment Works

If total founder pool is 85% equity shared among 5 co-founders, and the group agrees to collectively invest (e.g., $100K pre-seed):

Founder Equity Share* Pro-Rata Cash
Founder A (if 25%) 25% $29,400
Founder B (if 20%) 20% $23,500
Founder C (if 15%) 15% $17,600
Founder D (if 15%) 15% $17,600
Founder E (if 10%) 10% $11,800
Total 85% $100,000

*Illustrative equity splits. Actual splits to be agreed by co-founders.

What If a Founder Can't Pay Pro-Rata?

Options: (1) Other founders cover the shortfall → they gain proportional equity from the shortfall founder's share. (2) Convert the gap to a promissory note. (3) Accept dilution for that founder. Standard practice is Option 1 with a defined window (e.g., 60 days to cure).

Design Peeps & Ambeego: If They Also Invest Cash

The "Double-Dipping" Question

If Design Peeps (10% sweat equity) also invests, say, $20K cash — do they get additional equity on top of their 10%?

Yes — and this is fair. Here's why:

  • The 10% compensates past work (design deliverables already completed)
  • The cash investment compensates new capital at current valuation
  • These are two separate transactions — just like a CTO with equity who also invests in a round
  • The key protection is vesting on the sweat equity — if they stop working, unvested shares return

Example Math

Pre-money valuation $2M (illustrative)
Design Peeps sweat equity 10% (=$200K implied value)
Design Peeps cash investment $20K (=1% at $2M valuation)
Design Peeps total post-investment ~11% (10% sweat + 1% cash)
Ambeego sweat equity 5% (=$100K implied value)
Ambeego cash investment $10K (=0.5% at $2M valuation)
Ambeego total post-investment ~5.5% (5% sweat + 0.5% cash)

Bottom line: Sweat equity = compensation for past work. Cash equity = new investment. They're separate instruments. Vesting on sweat equity + standard investment terms for cash = no double-dipping.

Risk Register & Mitigations

HIGH Product fixes don't move PMF

We fix onboarding + paywall but PMF stays <40%. Users still see app as "nice-to-have."

Mitigation: Run rapid A/B tests with 2-week cohort windows. If PMF doesn't move by Q3, pivot feature priority to AI chat depth over breadth.

HIGH Seed round doesn't close at $850K

Investor appetite lower than expected. We raise less or take longer.

Mitigation: Founder bridge funding ($50-100K) to cover Q1-Q2. Lean hiring plan activates only with confirmed capital. $60K contingency reserve in budget.

MED CPI inflation in Tier-1 markets

CPIs rising as more wellness apps enter market. Our $1.80 Tier-1 target may be too optimistic.

Mitigation: Heavy organic/influencer investment (20% of budget). Shift spend to MENA where CPIs are 60% lower. University partnerships as zero-CPI channel.

MED Equity disagreement stalls progress

Design Peeps / Ambeego dispute resolution takes too long or creates founder conflict.

Mitigation: Resolve before seed round closes (hard deadline). Use Option A (vesting) as default. Bring in neutral advisor/lawyer if needed. Budget $18K for legal.

MED Pakistan hiring quality / retention

Top PK devs get poached by Gulf or remote US companies paying 3-5x.

Mitigation: Offer equity grants (0.25-0.5% vesting) to key hires. Pay at 75th percentile for PK market. Emphasize mission-driven culture and career growth.

LOW Guest mode increases anonymous churn

Users stay anonymous, never convert, can't be re-engaged.

Mitigation: Progressive signup nudges after 2 sessions. Gate premium + Echo behind account creation. Track guest→registered conversion rate.
