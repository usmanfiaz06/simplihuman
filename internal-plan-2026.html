Confidential Equity

Equity Structure: Resolving Sweat vs Cash

Two service partners (Design Peeps at 10% and Ambeego at 5%) hold equity for work contributions. Cash co-founders fund operations. We need a fair framework to balance sweat equity vs cash equity — ensuring service partners are not expected to also pay cash, and cash partners are not shortchanged.

The Core Problem

Fairness risk: Service partners contribute work against equity. Cash partners fund operations. If service partners were also expected to fund cash AND do work, they'd be over-contributing. Conversely, cash partners need assurance that equity given for work is proportional to actual delivery.

Industry Standard: Slicing Pie Model

Mike Moyer's Slicing Pie framework for resolving sweat vs cash in early-stage startups:

  • All contributions converted to "slices"
  • Cash contributions valued at 2-4x multiplier (cash is scarcer and riskier)
  • Sweat contributions valued at fair market rate × time
  • Each person's equity = their slices / total slices
  • Dynamic: Equity adjusts as new contributions are made

Recommended Resolution Options

A

Honor at Agreed Valuation (Recommended)

Honor equity at the valuation agreed when contracts were signed. Calculate proportionally to actual work delivered against the total agreed scope. Earned equity is locked; remaining equity continues to vest as work is delivered.

If partners also invest cash: Cash buys additional shares from a new option pool at current valuation — separate from sweat equity. No double-counting because sweat = past work, cash = new capital.
B

Convert to Contractor + Reduced Equity

Retrospectively value work at market rate. Convert to: reduced equity (proportional to work done) + cash payment for remainder. Clean break if they're not ongoing contributors.

C

Slicing Pie Full Recalculation

Recalculate all equity from scratch using fair market rates for all contributions (time, cash, IP). Most fair but most complex. Requires agreement from all parties on rates.

Key Principles (Any Option)

  • Cash gets a premium: 2-4x multiplier vs sweat (cash carries more risk)
  • Ongoing work ≠ free: Future work should be compensated separately
  • Service partners should not pay cash: Their contribution is work; expecting both is unfair
  • Get it in writing: Formal shareholders' agreement before any round

What If a Founder Can't Pay Pro-Rata?

Options: (1) Other founders cover the shortfall → they gain proportional equity from the shortfall founder's share. (2) Convert the gap to a promissory note. (3) Accept dilution for that founder. Standard practice is Option 1 with a defined window (e.g., 60 days to cure).

Design Peeps & Ambeego: If They Also Invest Cash

The "Double-Dipping" Question

If Design Peeps (10% sweat equity) also invests cash — do they get additional equity on top of their 10%?

Yes — and this is fair. Here's why:

  • The 10% compensates past work (design deliverables)
  • The cash investment compensates new capital at current valuation
  • These are two separate transactions
  • The key protection is that sweat equity is proportional to work delivered

Example Math

Pre-money valuation$2M (illustrative)
Design Peeps sweat equity10% (=$200K implied)
Design Peeps cash investment$20K (=1% at $2M)
Design Peeps total~11% (10% sweat + 1% cash)
Ambeego sweat equity5% (=$100K implied)
Ambeego cash investment$10K (=0.5% at $2M)
Ambeego total~5.5% (5% sweat + 0.5% cash)

Bottom line: Sweat equity = compensation for work. Cash equity = new investment. They're separate instruments. Proportional work-based sweat equity + standard investment terms for cash = no double-dipping.
