Each paid install costs $1.10, but only 2.8% convert — so effectively you’re paying $39.29 for each subscriber.

Why This Split Matters

Free users are not profitable on paid channels (LTV $0.54 vs CPI $1.10 = loss of $0.56 per user). They only make sense via organic growth ($0 CAC) or as a funnel to paid.
Paid subscribers are the revenue engine — CAC of $39.29 is tight at 1.10x LTV:CAC in Y1, but this improves to 3.3x by Y2 as product fixes increase conversion and reduce churn.
Implication: In early stages, focus on organic + Tier-2 (cheap) channels. Only scale Tier-1 spend after conversion improvements are proven post-PMF.

Bootstrapped Reality Check

Strengths: Low burn ($7K/mo avg), service partners absorb $62.8K in costs, B2B path to profitability without massive user scale, no dilution from investors.
Weaknesses: Cash runs out at Month 13 — need to bridge via additional partner cash or early B2B revenue. Growth is slow (27K users vs 97K funded). Cannot invest heavily in marketing. Team remains lean, limiting feature velocity.
Critical path: B2B revenue is the make-or-break factor. If 3+ B2B deals close by M10-M12 generating $2K+/mo, the bootstrapped path works. If B2B stalls, additional cash injection of $30–50K is needed by M10.
When to seek funding: If PMF hits 40%+ and B2B pilots validate, consider raising $500K–$850K to accelerate (see Path B below).
